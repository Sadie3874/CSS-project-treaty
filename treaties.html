Treaty 3 of Protection and Preservation

What is a Treaty?

A treaty is an agreement between nations, In Canada it is an agreement between the Government and the Indigenous people. Most of the time the treaties were made to share land or be able to cross the Indigenous peoples land along with using some resources. In return the Indigenous people got more land or more resources.

The Numbered Treaties

The numbered treaties are 11 treaties that stretch from Ontario to British Columbia. The numbered Treaties are agreements between the Indigenous peoples and the Crown. The Treaties gave the crown more land and in return the Indigenous people would get hunting and fishing tools, annual salary, farming supplies, land etc.

Points of View

An important thing to know is that the Crown and the Indigenous people viewed Treaties very differently, to the Indigenous people they saw the treaties as an agreement between nations vs the Crown saw treaties to get more land and power. Because of how the Crown viewed the Treaties many of the agreements between the 2 nations where never fulfilled. A lot of land that was promised to the Indigenous people was never given and are fighting to get the land to this day.
